eEngravers Old English

Typeface Information

Engravers Old English is a blackletter typeface created by Morris Fuller Benton for the American Type Founders (ATF) company in 1901. It is a sturdy and modernized version of the Old English style typeface, designed to mimic the engraved lettering on American social and business documents from the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

It was widely used for diplomas, certificates, legal deeds, newspaper mashheads, advertising, and letterheads due to its decorative and distinguished appearance.

While the typeface is primarily attributed to Benton. The original metal type was released by American Type Founders (ATF), which was a dominant U.S. manufacturer of metal type. A variation, Engravers Old English Open, was released by ATF in 1902, and a bold version was designed in 1910.
