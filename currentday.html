The typeface Engravers Old English is still used today for titles in newspaper publications like The New York Times, The Washington Post, and The Los Angeles Times. This intricate typeface is reserved exclusively for titling, rather than body text. It is reserved for titles because it is eye-catching, authoritative, and decorative. Crucially, it is never used for article body text because its elaborate forms severely restrict readability at smaller point sizes.
